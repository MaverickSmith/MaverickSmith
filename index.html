About Me

Your Photo

I am an Assistant Professor of Psychology and director of the Memory, Aging, and Vision (MAV) Lab. My research investigates how people perceive, segment, and remember everyday events, with a particular focus on cognitive aging, event cognition, visual attention, and memory.

Our lab uses behavioral methods, eye tracking, neuroimaging, and computational approaches to better understand how memory functions in everyday life.

Research Interests

Event Memory

Understanding how people encode and remember naturalistic events.

Event Segmentation

Investigating how individuals identify meaningful event boundaries.

Cognitive Aging

Examining age-related changes in perception, attention, and memory.

Vision & Attention

Studying how visual information guides memory formation.

Memory, Aging, and Vision (MAV) Lab

The MAV Lab explores how people understand and remember everyday experiences. We are particularly interested in event perception, aging, visual cognition, and memory interventions.

Interested students are encouraged to contact me about research opportunities.
